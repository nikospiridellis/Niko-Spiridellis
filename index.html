About Me

Sports Media Professional & Street Photographer

I'm Niko Spiridellis, a junior at NYU's Tisch Institute for Global Sport, where I study Sports Management with a minor in Spanish. My work lives at the intersection of sports, storytelling, and media—with a focus on capturing and sharing the moments that often go unnoticed.

I currently serve as Director of the NYU Sports Film Festival, a student-led international event that celebrates sports cinema and connects emerging creators from around the world with industry professionals. I lead all aspects of the festival—from programming and partnerships to marketing, budgeting, and producing an annual hour-long television special with SNY. The festival has grown into a dynamic platform at NYU, offering students hands-on experience while spotlighting stories that stretch far beyond the scoreboard.

Outside the sports world, I'm a street photographer. What started as a hobby has become a personal practice of documenting overlooked beauty in everyday life. From Los Angeles to New York, and after spending extensive time in Madrid, I've developed a focus grounded in spontaneity and the subtle details. It's my way of sharing my perspective by putting you behind my lens.

From global events to capturing the little moments on the street, I'm driven by storytelling in all its forms—and constantly looking for new ways to connect people through sport, culture, and art.
